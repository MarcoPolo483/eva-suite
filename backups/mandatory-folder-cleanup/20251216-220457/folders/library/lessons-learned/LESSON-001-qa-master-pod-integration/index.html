    [LESSON-001] QA Master Must Be Embedded in Pod Teams¶

    Pod: Cross-Pod (Alpha, Beta, Gamma, Delta) Role: Scrum Master & QA Master Sprint: Sprint-01 (Week 0 - Planning) Date: 2025-11-17 Tags: team-structure, qa-workflow, agile, process, pod-design

    üìã Context¶

    During Sprint 01 planning, we designed the EVA Agile Command team structure with 4 pods:

    • Pod Alpha (Foundation): eva-core, eva-auth, eva-agent ‚Üí ai2
    • Pod Beta (API & Integration): eva-api, eva-mcp, eva-openai ‚Üí ai2
    • Pod Gamma (Frontend & UX): eva-ui, eva-i11y, eva-i18n ‚Üí windowsGuy
    • Pod Delta (Platform Ops): eva-infra, eva-ops, eva-metering ‚Üí windowsGuy

    Initial design had QA Master (GitHub Copilot) as a separate coordination role outside the pod teams, reviewing PRs centrally.

    Problem recognized: Marco (Product Owner) identified that QA Master needed to be part of each pod, not separate from the team.

    ‚ùå Problem¶

    Symptom: QA Master role appeared disconnected from daily pod work, risking:

    • Bottleneck: All 4 pods waiting for single QA Master to review PRs
    • Lack of ownership: Dev Masters might view QA as "someone else's job"
    • Delayed feedback: QA happens at the end, not during development
    • Context loss: QA Master reviewing PRs without deep pod context

    Root Cause:

    • QA Master was defined as a central coordination function rather than an embedded team member
    • Pod structure diagrams showed Dev Masters only, no mention of QA role
    • Workflow unclear: Who fixes failed QA gates? Does QA Master fix or assign back?

    ‚úÖ Solution¶

    Redesigned QA Master role as embedded in all pods:

    Dev Master ‚Üí PR ‚Üí QA Gate ‚Üí QA Master Reviews

    Key Principle: QA Master does NOT fix issues directly. Dev Masters own their code quality.

    üìö Lesson Learned¶

    For Future Team Design¶

    1. Embed quality roles in teams, don't make them separate gates:

    2. ‚úÖ QA Master is part of the pod, attends stand-ups, knows context

    3. ‚ùå QA Master is external reviewer who only sees PRs

    4. Assign-back model prevents bottlenecks:

    5. QA reviews, approves, or assigns back with feedback

    6. Dev Masters fix their own issues (ownership + learning)

    7. QA doesn't become the fixer (avoids dependency)

    8. Clear workflow prevents confusion:

    9. Document who does what (review vs fix)

    10. Define success criteria (pass = merge, fail = assign back)

    11. Make it visible (sprint board columns: Ready ‚Üí In Progress ‚Üí QA Gate ‚Üí Done)

    12. One person, multiple pods is OK if:

    13. Capacity is planned (20h/sprint for PR reviews)
    14. SLA is defined (review within 4 hours for P1)
    15. Dev Masters help by following DoD before submitting

    When to Apply This Pattern¶

    • Multi-pod Agile teams with shared quality standards
    • Small teams where specialists must cover multiple areas
    • Fast-paced sprints needing quick feedback loops
    • Enterprise projects with strict quality gates (80% coverage, WCAG 2.1 AA, security scans)
