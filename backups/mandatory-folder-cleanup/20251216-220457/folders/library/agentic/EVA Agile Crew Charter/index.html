    EVA AI Agile Crew Charter¶

    Subtitle: How the EVA Agile Crew Builds and Evolves a Canada-Grade Agentic Platform Version: Draft 0.4 Date: 2025-11-21

    1. Purpose¶

    The EVA Agile Crew exists to design, build, and operate the EVA platform and its agentic capabilities in a way that:

    • Aligns with the Government of Canada Agentic AI Template (Volumes 0–4).
    • Delivers reliable, secure, accessible, bilingual services that are Canada-grade (federal, Protected B, audit-ready).
    • Demonstrates how humans plus AI agents can co-develop software safely and effectively, with traceability and guardrails.

    This Charter defines:

    • Team structure and roles for humans and EVA DevTools agents (P01–P15).
    • Ways of working, including ceremonies, artefacts, branching, CI/CD, UAT.
    • The requirements refinement process, including recursion and agent support.
    • The relationship between the EVA Agile Crew, Jarvis (EVA DevMaster), GitHub and Copilot as the main execution environment.

    2. Scope¶

    The EVA Agile Crew is responsible for the EVA Platform (“EVA Suite”), including:

    • Foundation services such as APIs, auth, logging, cost attribution, safety and policy hooks.
    • RAG and retrieval including ingestion, indexing, semantic and vector search.
    • Agent runtime and tools registry, including DevTools agents and external tools.
    • LiveOps and monitoring including dashboards, SLOs, alerts, kill switches.

    The Crew is also responsible for core EVA UIs:

    • EVA Chat.
    • EVA Domain Assistant.
    • LiveOps and admin consoles.
    • Shared component and accessibility or personalization libraries.

    The Crew establishes and maintains:

    • GC-grade agentic patterns, as defined in the Agentic Template and EVA P01–P15 agent pattern documents.
    • Jarvis (EVA DevMaster) as the orchestration layer for planning and execution.

    Out of scope, but influenced:

    • Program-specific policy and operational decisions.
    • Enterprise AI governance policy; EVA implements the technical, auditable realization of those policies.

    3. Pods, Repos and Ownership¶

    The EVA Agile Crew works in pods. Each pod is a stable, cross-functional group that owns one or more repositories and services. Pods are defined centrally in the pods map file and each repo has its own .eva-pod.yaml to declare its pod and agent patterns.

    3.1 Pod Categories¶

    • POD-F – Foundation and Platform.
    • POD-X – Experience and UI.
    • POD-O – Operations and LiveOps.
    • POD-S-* – Solution pods such as Jurisprudence, AssistMe and others.

    Each pod:

    • Owns specific repos and services.
    • Follows common ways of working from this Charter.
    • Uses the DevTools agents that match its responsibilities.

    3.2 Current Pods and Repos¶

    POD-F – Foundation and Platform

    • Owns APIM integration, Cosmos and data layer, platform-wide docs and Jarvis orchestration.
    • Current repos include eva-apim-integration, eva-cosmos-enhancements, eva-meta and optional eva-documentation.

    POD-X – Experience and UI

    • Owns EVA DA, EVA Chat, shared components, accessibility and personalization.
    • Current repos include eva-da-ms-pubsec, and later eva-ui.

    POD-O – Operations and LiveOps

    • Owns automation scripts, time tracking, local dev helpers and LiveOps automation.
    • Current repos include eva-automation-scripts, and later eva-ops or eva-liveops-console.

    POD-S-Juris and POD-S-Assist (future pods)

    • Own solution-specific APIs, RAG and UIs.
    • Planned repos include juris-api, juris-rag, juris-ui, assist-api, assist-rag, assist-ui.

    These solution pods can be reserved in the pods map even before repos exist so Jarvis understands the pattern.

    3.3 .eva-pod.yaml per Repo¶

    Each repo has a small manifest file with:

    • Pod identifier.
    • Service name.
    • Primary agent patterns such as REQ, DES, COD, TST, CICD, SEC, UXA, RUN, MET, DVM.

    Jarvis and housekeeping scripts keep .eva-pod.yaml aligned with the central pods map.

    4. Human Roles and Responsibilities¶

    Platform Service Owner (crew-wide)

    • Accountable for EVA as a service, including SLOs, roadmap and risk posture.
    • Aligns EVA roadmap to departmental priorities and GC agentic templates.

    Product Owner (per pod)

    • Owns the pod backlog and priorities.
    • Balances value, risk and capacity.
    • Ensures alignment to agentic patterns, this Charter and governance decisions.

    Tech Lead or Architect (per pod)

    • Owns technical design within pod scope.
    • Ensures code, APIs and architecture follow EVA standards and reuse shared patterns.
    • Co-authors ADRs with other pod leads when changes cross pods.

    Scrum Master or Flow Facilitator

    • Facilitates ceremonies such as stand-ups, planning, reviews and retrospectives.
    • Helps manage dependencies and removes blockers.
    • Protects focus and sustainable flow.

    Developers

    • Implement features end-to-end including tests, docs and code.
    • Use Copilot, EVA DevTools agents and Jarvis responsibly.
    • Participate in reviews, ADRs and continuous improvement.

    DevOps and SRE

    • Own CI and CD pipelines and environment automation.
    • Integrate logging, metrics, alerts and LiveOps hooks.

    AI Steward (crew-wide)

    • Guards responsible AI use inside the SDLC.
    • Ensures DevTools agents operate with clear scope, logging and kill switches.
    • Liaises with AI governance, privacy and security functions.

    Senior Advisor (external LLM)

    • Acts as an optional, explicitly invoked advisor for complex reasoning, strategy, requirements and design.
    • Is used through controlled prompts and patterns defined in EVA P02 and related docs.
    • Never has direct write access to code or configuration; outputs always pass through human and DevTools checks.

    5. EVA DevTools Agents and Patterns¶

    EVA DevTools agents follow the patterns defined in EVA P01–P15. Key patterns include:

    • P01 – Agent Patterns and Toolbox Roadmap.
    • P02 – Requirements Agent.
    • P03 – Scrum or SM Agent.
    • P04 – Repo Librarian Agent.
    • P05 – Scaffolder Agent.
    • P06 – Review Agent.
    • P07 – Testing and Failure Explainer Agent.
    • P08 – CI/CD Guardian Agent.
    • P09 – Runbook and SRE Agent.
    • P10 – Metrics and Insight Agent.
    • P11 – Security and Compliance Helper Agent.
    • P12 – UX, Copy and Accessibility Agent.
    • P13 – Onboarding and Sandbox Coach Agent.
    • P14 – DevOps LiveOps Companion Agent.
    • P15 – Dev Master Orchestrator Agent (Jarvis).

    General principles:

    • Agents never merge code or change production configuration directly.
    • Agents act through visible artefacts such as comments, suggested files or PR reviews.
    • All agent activity is traceable to a specific agent identity and execution context.
    • Agents are subject to logging, kill switches and audit, consistent with EVA governance and GC guidance.

    6. Ways of Working¶

    Daily Stand-up

    • Held per pod.
    • Focus on progress, blockers and immediate coordination.
    • Optionally note where DevTools agents helped or created friction.

    Backlog Refinement

    • Held at least once per sprint.
    • Product Owner, Tech Lead, key developers and, when useful, the Scrum Agent and Requirements Agent.
    • Uses the refinement levels and recursion rules from Section 7.

    Sprint or Flow Planning

    • Agree on sprint or flow goals.
    • Pull only items that meet Definition of Ready.
    • Confirm pod and repo ownership for each item.

    Review and Demo

    • Show working software, not only documentation.
    • Demonstrate reuse of EVA patterns and DevTools agents where relevant.

    Retrospective

    • Reflect on what worked and what did not.
    • Include specific observations about Jarvis, Copilot and DevTools usage.
    • Capture improvement actions as explicit issues or epics.

    Artefacts

    • GitHub Issues and Projects for tracking work.
    • CDDs and canvases for major features and solutions.
    • ADRs for significant architecture decisions.
    • LiveOps dashboards, reports and incident records.
    • DevMaster and DevTools orchestration logs.

    Definition of Ready

    • Problem and value are clear.
    • Scope is small enough for one pod to deliver within one sprint.
    • Basic acceptance criteria exist, even if refined later.
    • Dependencies and key risks are identified.
    • Impacted repos and pods are known.

    Definition of Done

    • Code changes build successfully in CI.
    • Tests are updated or added and pass.
    • Linting and formatting checks pass.
    • Docs are updated where needed, including ADRs and runbooks when applicable.
    • At least one human approval plus required automated checks.
    • Deployed to the appropriate environment and validated for that stage.

    7. Requirements Refinement and Recursion¶

    Levels of refinement:

    • Level 0 – Idea or Opportunity, expressed in free text.
    • Level 1 – Concept Canvas (Agent or Feature canvas tied to Volumes 1 and 4).
    • Level 2 – Epics and capabilities, including key non-functional requirements.
    • Level 3 – Implementation-ready stories and tasks a pod can complete in one sprint.

    Recursion rule:

    • If an item cannot be completed by one pod in one sprint as one or two PRs, or lacks pod, repo, estimate or acceptance criteria, it must be refined into smaller child items.
    • Refinement continues until items can be flagged as ready-for-sprint.

    Human and agent collaboration:

    • The Requirements Agent and Senior Advisor can propose epics, stories, acceptance criteria and test ideas from CDDs and canvases.
    • Humans validate, adjust and add constraints from policy, infra and organizational reality.
    • Items planned for the next sprint must reach Level 3.
    • Longer-term or uncertain items can remain at Level 1 or Level 2 and be revisited.

    8. Jarvis, GitHub, Copilot and Senior Advisor¶

    Jarvis (Dev Master Orchestrator, P15) is implemented using GitHub, GitHub Actions and Copilot. It runs two main pipelines:

    Planning pipeline

    • Starts from CDDs and high-level ideas.
    • Uses the Requirements Agent and, when needed, the Senior Advisor.
    • Produces epics, stories and sprints/sprint-XXX.yaml sprint configs.

    Execution pipeline

    • Uses sprint configs, pods map and .eva-pod.yaml manifests.
    • Coordinates human work and Copilot coding work.
    • Triggers CI, CD, UAT and reporting workflows.

    Jarvis can use the external Senior Advisor model as follows:

    • For deep reasoning or cross-cutting design, Jarvis can call the Senior Advisor through a clearly labelled, bounded pattern.
    • Inputs are limited to non-sensitive context and existing repo artefacts.
    • Outputs are treated as proposals and must be reviewed and accepted by humans and DevTools agents.
    • Jarvis must always be able to fall back to local and GitHub-only resources when external access is not available or not allowed.

    9. Governance, Risk and Compliance¶

    The EVA Agile Crew:

    • Follows TBS and departmental guidance on generative AI, security and privacy.
    • Ensures EVA platform features enforce security classification and access controls.
    • Ensures DevTools agents and the Senior Advisor operate within approved scopes and are logged and auditable.
    • Produces governance artefacts such as trust posture summaries, ATO support materials and MTI-like views as needed.

    10. Continuous Improvement of This Charter¶

    • This Charter lives in Git and evolves via pull requests.
    • At least once per quarter, the crew reviews: pods and repo boundaries, ceremonies, DevTools usage, Jarvis behaviour and Senior Advisor integration.
    • Changes are proposed as issues and PRs, then communicated across pods after approval.
