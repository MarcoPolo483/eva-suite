    Government of Canada Agentic AI Template¶

    Volume 0 – Overview & Adoption Guide¶

    Subtitle: How Departments Can Use This Template to Build & Govern AI Agents
    Version: Draft 0.1
    Date: YYYY-MM-DD

    1. What This Package Is¶

    The Government of Canada Agentic AI Template is a reusable, technology-neutral set of documents that any federal department can adopt to:

    • Design and operate internal AI assistants and agents safely.
    • Align AI-enabled solutions with GC expectations for:
    • Security and privacy
    • Accessibility and bilingualism
    • Governance, accountability, and traceability

    It is not a product or a specific platform implementation.
    Instead, it is a reference template you can:

    • Fill in for your department (Instantiation Headers).
    • Tailor to your governance and technical reality.
    • Use to guide projects and conversations with partners.

    2. Who This Is For¶

    • CIO / CDO / Digital & IT Leaders
      Want a coherent way to talk about “our AI assistant / agent platform” across the department and with other departments.

    • Program & Business Owners
      Want to use AI agents internally (knowledge assistants, drafting aides, case summarizers) without breaching trust or policy.

    • AI Governance, Privacy, Security, Legal
      Need a common vocabulary and set of expectations for reviewing AI agent proposals.

    • Platform, Cloud, and Dev Teams
      Need concrete requirements and engineering patterns to build a reusable agent platform instead of one-off bots.

    3. What’s in the 4 Main Volumes¶

    Volume 1 – Platform & Agentic Requirements (Template)¶

    • Defines what the Platform must do:
    • Project spaces, agents, autonomy levels.
    • Directory/registry of projects and agents.
    • Security, accessibility, bilingualism, observability.
    • Tagged as:
    • [CORE] – recommended minimum for any dept.
    • [ADV] – advanced capabilities for mature teams.

    Think of Volume 1 as your architecture and requirement baseline.

    Volume 2 – Engineering & Delivery Guide (Template)¶

    • Defines how to build it:
    • Repo organization, dev environments (dev containers, Codespaces/remote dev).
    • Branching and PR practices.
    • CI/CD pipelines and quality gates.
    • Security scanning, dependency hygiene.
    • AI-assisted development guidance (e.g., use of Copilot-type tools).

    Volume 2 is your engineering playbook for the Platform and its services.

    Volume 3 – LiveOps & Governance Model (Template)¶

    • Defines how to run and govern it in production:
    • Environments and SLOs.
    • Monitoring and LiveOps console.
    • Logging and traceability.
    • Incident management, kill switch, degraded modes.
    • AI-specific governance (autonomy, lifecycle tiers, trust posture).

    Volume 3 is your operations and governance handbook.

    Volume 4 – Use Cases, Patterns & Templates (Application Guide)¶

    • Defines how to apply all this to real work:
    • Common federal agent archetypes (Knowledge Explorer, Drafting Assistant, Case Context Summarizer, etc.).
    • Safe design patterns (“Search + Explain”, “Draft, Don’t Decide”, etc.).
    • Anti-patterns (black-box decision-maker, policy oracle, shadow agent).
    • Practical templates:
      • Agent Concept Canvas.
      • Governance checklists.
      • Design review questions.

    Volume 4 is your toolkit for designing and reviewing agent use cases.

    4. How a Department Adopts This Template (Quick Start)¶

    You can adopt this package in five pragmatic steps:

    Step 1 – Instantiate the Template¶

    For each Volume (1–4):

    • Fill the Instantiation Header at the top:
    • {{Department Name}}
    • {{Platform Name}} or “to be defined”
    • Governance bodies (AI, privacy, security)
    • Max permitted classification (e.g., up to Protected B)

    Decide:

    • Which [CORE] items you will adopt immediately.
    • Which [ADV] items are “future state” for your roadmap.

    Step 2 – Map to Your Governance & ITSM¶

    With AI governance, privacy, security, and ITSM leads:

    • Map template roles to real org roles (e.g., Platform Service Owner = Director, Platforms & Integration).
    • Map:
    • “AI Governance Body” → your AI review committee or equivalent.
    • Incident and change processes → your ITSM/ITIL tools and workflows.

    Document:

    • How Sandbox → Pilot → Production maps to your current release and risk processes.

    Step 3 – Choose a Small Starter Catalogue of Use Cases¶

    Use Volume 4 to pick 2–3 low- to medium-risk internal use cases, like:

    • Internal Knowledge Explorer for policy docs.
    • Drafting Assistant for internal emails and notes.
    • Case Context Summarizer using de-identified or low-risk data.

    For each:

    • Fill an Agent Concept Canvas.
    • Decide:
    • Archetype & pattern.
    • Autonomy level (start Advisory/Assistive).
    • Lifecycle tier (start in Sandbox or Pilot).

    These become your pilot agents.

    Step 4 – Stand Up or Map to a Platform¶

    If you already have an AI assistant platform (or multiple):

    • Use Volume 1 to map existing components to the template:
    • Where do project spaces and agents live?
    • How are logs and monitoring handled?
    • Where are kill switches and autonomy settings implemented?

    If you don’t have one:

    • Use Volumes 1 & 2 to define a minimal viable Platform:
    • One chat UI + one RAG backend.
    • One directory/registry (even if it’s a database + simple admin UI).
    • One logging pipeline + basic dashboards.

    Your starter use cases from Step 3 live on this Platform.

    Step 5 – Run Pilots, Learn, and Iterate¶

    For each pilot:

    • Apply Volume 3:
    • Set SLOs (simple ones are fine).
    • Enable monitoring and kill switch.

    • Define incident workflows (who does what).

    • Use Volume 4 templates to:

    • Capture risks and mitigations.
    • Collect feedback.

    Then:

    • Decide whether to:
    • Scale (Pilot → Production).
    • Adjust scope/autonomy.
    • Retire the agent and document lessons.

    5. How to Use This Template for Interdepartmental Sharing¶

    If you want this to be usable across GC, you can:

    1. Publish the set internally (e.g., GCcollab / GCwiki / repo):
    2. Volume 0 → landing page / README.

    3. Volumes 1–4 → detailed docs.

    4. Add Annexes that departments can ignore or reuse:

    5. Annex A – Example mapping from a real platform (e.g., how ESDC’s “EVA” maps to Vol. 1).
    6. Annex B – Example RACI for roles (Platform Owner, AI Governance, Privacy, Security, Program).

    7. Annex C – Example intake form or self-assessment (can be based on your AICOE forms).

    8. Socialize with:

    9. CIO Council, DG forums, AI governance committees.
    10. As a “starting point template” rather than a standard.

    6. How This Connects to Other GC Work¶

    This template is meant to sit under existing GC policy, not replace it.
    Each department should:

    • Link references in the headers to:
    • GC AI guidance and directives.
    • Departmental privacy and security policies.
    • Digital / data strategies.

    It is deliberately:

    • Cloud/vendor-neutral (no platform lock-in).
    • Focused on internal AI agents first, not public-facing automation.
    • Compatible with:
    • Human accountability principles.
    • Risk-based approaches to AI.

    7. Suggested Next Actions for Your Department¶

    For your immediate context, a simple action list:

    1. Drop all 5 Volumes into a repo (e.g., gc-agentic-ai-template):
    2. Volume 0–4 as .md files.
    3. Add an Annex A – EVA Mapping (for ESDC):
    4. Map “the Platform” → EVA.
    5. Map abstract components → concrete EVA components (EVA Chat, EVA DA, APIM, logging stacks, etc.).
    6. Bring Vol 0 + one page of diagrams to your next DG/ADM conversation:
    7. Use it as the story spine: “Here’s the GC-grade template we’re building EVA against.”
    8. Use Volume 4 + Agent Canvas to document 2–3 EVA agent use cases:
    9. Jurisprudence.
    10. AssistMe migration / KM.
    11. CPP-D case summarizer.
    12. Start aligning your existing AICOE intake forms with the Agent Concept Canvas & governance checklist so everything ties together.
